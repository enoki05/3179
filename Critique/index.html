⚒️ Exploring Australian Employment ⚒️

As the rise in concerns of the future of employment continues, this visualisation seeks to grasp the present and emerging trends in Australian employment.

📍 Recruitment Rates by Region 📍

The recruitment data reveals significant regional variation, with the Northern Territory consistently showing the highest recruitment rates throughout the period, peaking at 0.76 in September 2022, while New South Wales maintained the lowest rates below 60%.

📈 Company Recruitment Insights Over Time 📈

Use the slider to explore company recruitment insights for the selected year.

The amount of new positions was very high in 2020 likely due to the COVID-19 Pandemic. From then on, staff turnover grew and became a big reason for recruitment. There has also been a growth in undesirable and inexperienced workers applying for positions compared to previous years and many more people are applying to jobs in 2025. The number of stages in the recruitment process has also increased over time, indicating a more rigorous hiring process by companies likely due to increased competition.

🌍 Employment by Region & Labour Market Ratings 🌍

Explore the map and heatmap below to view employment data by region and labour market ratings over time.

Sydney's employment landscape is dominated by the Inner South West region with 338,100 employed persons and an impressive employment-to-population ratio of 77.9%, while Melbourne's South East leads Victoria with similar strong employment metrics. The heatmap reveals temporal patterns in labour market performance across different regions, highlighting areas with strong and weak labour market conditions.

📊 Suitability of Job Applicants 📊

The number of applicants has risen since 2020 indicating stronger competition, but the suitability of applicants has decreased. In 2025, roughly 16% of applicants are deemed suitable for a job, whereas in 2020, 22% were suitable. This indicates a growing skills mismatch in the labour market.

🔮 2029 Projected Count and Salary of Different Jobs 🔮

Click on the industry dropdown to filter data by industry and explore projected job counts and salaries for 2029.

Professional, scientific, and technical services are projected to have the highest number of jobs in 2029, totaling 1,229,000 positions, while the agriculture, forestry, and fishing sector is expected to have the lowest. In terms of salary, Oil and Gas Extraction is projected to have the highest salary, while food and beverages having the lowest with lot's of people having a job in this area.
