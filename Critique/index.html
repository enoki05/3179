⚒️ Exploring Australian Employment ⚒️

As the rise in concerns of the future of employment continues, this visualisation seeks to grasp the present and emerging trends in Australian employment.

The inner areas of Australia are on the lower end of employment due to businesses being predominantly located in outer regions, leading to a higher concentration of jobs there. This trend is particularly evident in metropolitan areas where outer suburbs host a significant number of employment opportunities.

Labour Market Ratings

Sydney's employment landscape is dominated by the Inner South West region with 338,100 employed persons and an impressive employment-to-population ratio of 77.9%, while Melbourne's South East leads Victoria with similar strong employment metrics. The heatmap reveals temporal patterns in labour market performance across different regions.

📊 Employment Growth Projections 📊

Healthcare and Social Assistance is projected to become Australia's largest employer by 2029 with 2,544,500 workers, representing a 12.5% increase from 2024 and reflecting the aging population's impact on workforce demand. Electricity, Gas, Water and Waste Services is expected to see the lowest growth projections of 197,000 workers.

⚖️ Job Competition and Skills Mismatch ⚖️

The labour market has experienced a dramatic shift in competition for positions, with total applicants per vacancy doubling from 13.5 in July 2021 to 27.5 in July 2025, indicating significantly increased job competition. Roughly about 16% of applicants that apply are hired in 2025.
